Western Cordillera and The Interior Plains - Comparison

Written by Hayden Kitchen, 2019-10-15

The Western Cordillera

The Western Cordillera is the westmost landform region of Canada. Provinces that are included in the region include: British Columbia, Yukon, Alberta, and the Northwest Territories. Western Cordillera was created near the end of the Mesozoic Era through the beginning of the Cenozoic Era.

Physical Features:

  • Rivers
  • Mountains
  • Lakes
  • Forests
  • Igneous, Sedimentary, and Metamorphic Rock

The Interior Plains

The Interior Plains is the second westmost landform region in Canada. Provinces that are included in this region include: Northwest Territories, Columbia, Alberta, Saskatchawan, and Manitoba. This landform region was formed from the Precambrian Era to the Cenozoic Era.

Physical Features:

  • Hills
  • Cliffs
  • Wide river valleys
  • Forests
  • Low-lying mountains

Ecozone Project - Mixedwood Plains

Vegetation

Vast tracks of forest once blanketed most of this ecozone. Unfortunately, due to deforestation, construction and the increase of population, the amount of trees have drastically reduced.The removal of vegetation is bad because it destroys the habitats for wildlife, forcing the animals to adjust to the new man-made surroundings, which is not only bad for the animals, but for us as well.

Some examples of plant species this ecozone includes is:

  • Mixed forests
  • Maple trees
  • Sedges and moss
  • Meadow grasses
  • White pine

The soil in the Mixedwood Plains are leached, but is ideal for this region. This soil is especially great for growing crops such as corn, different types of beans such as soybeans, tobacco, and other crops.
